# How to Quickly Organize a Messy Desk? 5 Scientific Methods to Double Your Efficiency

Do you often stare at a messy desk in a daze? Every time you want to work or study, just cleaning the desk takes up most of your time?

Scientific research shows that cluttered work environments significantly reduce human cognitive ability and work efficiency. A study from Princeton University found that messy environments distract attention, reducing work efficiency by up to 40%.

The good news is that with the right methods, organizing your desk can be simple and efficient.

## 1. Photo Recognition, Crystal Clear

Before organizing, take a panoramic photo of your desk. This not only helps you globally assess the current level of chaos but also serves as a "before and after" comparison showcase.

Now with AI technology, you can directly upload photos and let AI analyze each item's category and optimal placement location. **TidySnap** is built on this concept—letting AI directly annotate on the photo where each item should go, making everything clear at a glance.

## 2. Item Categorization: High/Low Frequency/Archive

Categorize items based on usage frequency:

- **High-frequency items**: Used daily (pens, notebooks, phone stands, etc.) → Place within easy reach
- **Low-frequency items**: Used weekly (stapler, glue, etc.) → Store in slightly distant containers
- **Archived items**: Rarely used (old documents, souvenirs, etc.) → Unified storage in drawers or cabinets

## 3. Visualize Your Organization Plan

Traditional text-based organization lists are easily forgotten. In contrast, visual organization instructions are more intuitive and effective.

You can:
- Annotate each area's storage function on the photo
- Use different colors to distinguish item categories
- Save the ideal organized state as reference

## 4. Build the "Put Back Immediately" Habit

The biggest enemy of organization isn't mess—it's "putting things anywhere." Spending 5 seconds to put items back after each use is the key to maintaining cleanliness.

Set phone reminders to check every 1-2 hours if any items on your desk are "homeless."

## 5. Regular Review and Optimization

Spend 10 minutes each week reviewing:
- Which areas have become messy again?
- Which storage locations are unreasonable?
- What new items need to be stored?

Continuously optimize your storage solutions based on actual situations to maintain long-term cleanliness.
